But a funny thing happened right before they flew to Brazil. The special camera Cosmos ordered for Ben to use for filming the movie scenes still hadn't arrived two days before their scheduled flights! Ben called the camera rental company asking where the camera was that Cosmos had ordered. The guy was shocked that Cosmos was a real guy!  He thought it was a hoax order assuming ‘Cosmos’ couldn't be a real person’s name!  A camera was overnighted immediately.  

Ben and Cosmos flew to Sao Paulo, Brazil where they filmed and directed a beautiful movie titled, *‘Fly Away Beetle’*. The synchronicities that happened throughout the filming had a major effect on the making of this movie. Locals would suddenly appear and stop them from entering unsafe alleyways or side streets. They were being safely looked after! In addition,without planning beforehand, they had serendipitous chance encounters with the top three renowned Capoeira Masters in Brazil! All of them agreed to be interviewed for the movie.  Plus the young man who transforms his life through this art form. This film explores the history and cultural significance of Capoeira, an Afro-Brazilian martial art that combines elements of dance, acrobatics, and music.

Looking back, this desire to capture images on film was present in Ben since childhood. His father, a law professor, would take college law students to other countries to study their legal systems during the summers. By the time Ben was 20, he had traveled to 26 other countries with his family during his father’s teaching trips.

Ben’s favorite thing that excited him the most, especially during these trips, was venturing out and exploring areas with his camera. He could be inside an art museum or outside at an ocean coast. He always had his camera with him wherever he went. His family couldn't understand where this interest in photography came from. He seemed to be the only member of his family with this strong interest.  

I met Ben years before he filmed his movie when he came to help me edit videos regarding afterschool programs for grade schools in Eugene, Oregon. I founded a workshop class called, ‘Happiness Exposed’ where kids would explore ways to follow what naturally interested them and made them happy. When I saw how great Ben’s videos looked, I went to a local company and asked the owner if our class could produce an ad for tv for them. I wanted a real class experience! The owner said yes. Ben was surprised and happy about this as were all the kids.  

The result was a TV ad for Wild Plum Pies that highlighted their very huge selection of pies followed by 15 seconds dedicated to the after school classes in the BEST Program. I called it a ‘hybrid ad’ because it showed what kids could learn in an afterschool program while also advertising all the sweet pies offered by a local pie company.  The kids were the crew! 

Around a dozen PSA’s (Public Service Announcements) were made and the class was  renamed, ‘Just Kidding Productions’. The PSA’s appeared on Comcast TV to the excitement of the students.

Ben then expanded and made TV commercials in Portland after the school systems lost funding for the Arts programs. This preceded his filming his ‘Fly Away Beetle’ movie in Brazil.

Perhaps the most surprising news in his life was from his aunt after showing her the PSA’s from the after school workshops.  She told him that his Uncle was Haskell Wexler, one of film history’s ten most influential cinematographers, as well as being renowned as one of the most inventive cinematographers in Hollywood. So here was a connection after all!  Ben was thrilled to learn this and did meet Uncle Haskell after learning about him.  Ben was born to hold a camera!  All he had to do was follow what excited him!
